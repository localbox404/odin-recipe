Homemade Pasta Bolognese

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour
  • Eggs
  • Ground beef
  • Ground pork
  • Onion
  • Carrot
  • Celery
  • Garlic
  • Canned diced tomatoes or tomato paste
  • Red wine (optional)
  • Beef or chicken broth
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper
  • Dried herbs such as oregano, basil, and thyme
  • Bay leaf
  • Milk or cream (optional)
  • Freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil or parsley (for garnish)

Steps

Step 1: Prepare the Homemade Pasta

  • On a clean work surface, mound 2 cups of all-purpose flour and create a well in the center.
  • Crack 3 eggs into the well and lightly beat them with a fork.
  • Gradually incorporate the flour into the eggs until a dough forms.
  • Knead the dough for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  • Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for 30 minutes.
  • After resting, roll out the dough thinly using a rolling pin or pasta machine.
  • Cut the rolled-out dough into your desired shape for the pasta (e.g., fettuccine, tagliatelle).

Step 2: Prepare the Bolognese Sauce

  • Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large pot over medium heat.
  • Add 1 finely chopped onion, 1 diced carrot, and 1 diced celery stalk. Cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes.
  • Add 2 cloves of minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  • Increase the heat to medium-high and add 1/2 pound of ground beef and 1/2 pound of ground pork. Cook until browned, breaking up any large chunks with a spoon.
  • Stir in 1 can of diced tomatoes or 2 tablespoons of tomato paste, along with 1/2 cup of red wine (optional).
  • Add 1 cup of beef or chicken broth, 1 bay leaf, and 1 teaspoon each of dried oregano, basil, and thyme.
  • Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  • Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the sauce simmer for at least 1 hour, stirring occasionally.
  • If the sauce becomes too thick, you can add a splash of milk or cream to thin it out.

Step 3: Cook the Homemade Pasta

  • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  • Carefully add the homemade pasta to the boiling water and cook until al dente, usually 2-3 minutes for fresh pasta.
  • Drain the cooked pasta, reserving a cup of the pasta water.

Step 4: Combine the Pasta and Sauce

  • Add the cooked pasta to the pot with the Bolognese sauce.
  • Toss the pasta and sauce together until the pasta is evenly coated.
  • If the sauce seems too thick, you can add some of the reserved pasta water to loosen it up.

Step 5: Serve

  • Divide the Homemade Pasta Bolognese among serving plates or bowls.
  • Garnish with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and chopped fresh basil or parsley.
  • Serve immediately and enjoy your delicious Homemade Pasta Bolognese!
